RoboNation RoboSub Competition

RoboSub Official Website

What is the competition?

The Robosub competition is an exciting underwater robotics program in which teams of high school and college students from around the world design and build AUVs. These vehicles are designed to autonomously navigate through a series of tasks laid out in the challenge course. These tasks mimic ongoing research in autonomous underwater systems.

What do the vehicles do?

Some of the tasks include passing through gates, manipulating buoys, identifying colors and shapes, gathering and dispensing objects, sorting objects, following pipelines, launching projectiles, finding an acoustic pingers, and more. With each annual competition comes a new theme with a different set of build tasks.

Where is it held?

The TRANSDEC facility in San Diego, California has hosted the competition in recent years with the organization of RoboNation. TRANSDEC a naval testing pool, designed to be anechoic to simulate an unbounded body of water, making it ideal for reproducing conditions of ocean waters.

TRANSDEC Anechoic Pool in San Diego, CA

What WE do

Every year, the AVBotz team works year-round to prepare a capable AUV, construct task props, connect with sponsors, and document our entire process. By August, we're ready to drive down to San Diego to compete with and have fun with the teams at the competition. In an event filled with so many distinguished schools and engineering teams, we try our best to meet the caliber of engineering skill, even as a high school team. As we've seen in recent years, more and more teams—both university and high school—are joining RoboSub and each year we hope to learn and share ideas with each of them at comp.

Even though this year's in-person event has been cancelled, we're happy to continue talking with teams on the forums and are excited to watch the team videos for this year! We hope that everyone is staying safe and healthy out there!

WHo participates?

In 2019, more than 40 teams from around the world including the US, India, Singapore, Russia, Turkey, Japan, China, Canada, Poland, Brazil, Egypt, Norway, Puerto Rico, and Thailand came together to compete in the TRANSDEC Anechoic Pool to show their mastery of engineering, mission design, and autonomous robotics.
